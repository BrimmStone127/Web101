Pioneers of the Internet

Vannevar Bush

Vannever Bush was an scientist known in the computer industry for leading the way into the development of analog computers with his idea of a hypothetical microfilm viewer Memex. The fundamental concepts of Memex would be the foundation to modern hypertext.

Doug Englebart

Doug Englebart was an engineer and scientist best known for his work in HCI. He is known for the creation of the computer mouse and the development of hypertext. He also worked on creating the precursors to graphical user interface and was the presentor of the "Mother of all demos" which was a demonstration in 1968 of many fundamental parts of modern computing including: windows, hypertext, graphics, navigation, command input, video conferencing, word processing, and the computer mouse.

Ted Nelson

Ted Nelson founded project Xanadu with the goal of creating a simple to use interface and he is credited with coining the terms hypertext and hypermedia.

Steve Wozniak/Steve Jobs

Steve Wozniak and Steve Jobs are the co-founder of Apple computers. Steve Wozniak is credited with building the original Apple I computer and primarily designing the Apple II, he also is credited with inventing the universal remote. Steve Jobs helped create Apple alongside Wozniak and focused on selling the Apple I computers and focusing on the business end of the company. He became the CEO of Apple and was fundamental in making it the corporation it is today.

Tim Berners-Lee

Tim Berners-Lee is known as the inventor of the World Wide Web. He also is the director of the World Wide Web Consortium (W3C). The first hypertext website was hosted from a NeXT computer at CERN and it contained information on the project and is still visible today.

Marc Andreessen/Eric Bina

Marc Andreessen and Eric Bina are the creators of Mosaic which was the first web browser with a Graphical User Interface.

Rob Hartill

Rob Hartill is the creator of the Apache web server and the Internet Movie Database IMDB.

Lou Montulli

Lou Montulli is the programmer known for the creation of Lynx one of the first web browsers and the oldest that is still in use today.

Vint Cerf

Recognized as one of the fathers of the internet Vint Cerf helped create TCP/IP and was fundamental to the invention of electronic mail.

Bob Kahn

Bob Kahn is an electrical engineer who co-created the Transmission Control Protocol and the Internet Protocol (TCP/IP).
